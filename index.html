Selected projects

  • Conversational agent for operational protocol search
    Healthcare AI RAG Knowledge systems

    Designed and developed a conversational assistant to help healthcare operational teams quickly access internal protocols and procedures, reducing search time and improving consistency in service interactions.

    The project focused on building a structured RAG knowledge system tailored to healthcare call center operations, where fast and reliable access to internal guidance directly affects service quality.

    The work involved document ingestion pipelines, protocol structure extraction, chunking strategies aligned with operational procedures, metadata design and semantic indexing of large collections of internal documentation.

    Particular attention was given to retrieval quality, contextual relevance and traceability of answers, ensuring that responses could be linked back to specific protocol sections when used in operational contexts.

    The result was a production-oriented knowledge workflow designed to reduce operational friction and make protocol-based decisions more consistent across service teams.

  • Machine learning for missed appointment prediction
    Machine learning Healthcare operations Predictive analytics

    Built predictive models to identify high-risk missed appointments, enabling proactive operational actions to reduce no-show rates and improve resource utilization.

    The project started with an extensive discovery phase focused on understanding the operational context of appointment scheduling and the business implications of missed consultations.

    This stage included exploratory analysis of historical appointment data, identification of behavioral and temporal patterns, investigation of operational workflows and discussions with domain stakeholders to understand how predictions could realistically support decision-making.

    Based on this understanding, feature engineering strategies were designed to capture temporal patterns, appointment characteristics, patient history signals and contextual operational factors.

    Multiple machine learning approaches were evaluated and compared, focusing not only on predictive performance but also on interpretability and operational usability of the resulting models.

    The project was designed to support operational planning with earlier risk signals, helping teams intervene before missed appointments turn into wasted capacity.

  • Machine learning for dialysis prescription support
    Machine learning Clinical decision support Research

    Undergraduate research project focused on developing machine learning models to support dialysis prescription decisions based on clinical variables.

    The research explored the use of structured clinical data to assist treatment-related decision-making in dialysis contexts.

    The work combined exploratory data analysis, feature engineering, model development and evaluation of predictive performance in a clinically relevant scenario.
